Dom

I got my Mantis Mirror this past fall through their Kickstarter program. For a bit of background, I’m a recreational road cyclist and have previously used other helmet mirrors, but wasn’t happy with the clarity or the vibration aspects.

The biggest issue I’ve found with other mirrors is having to adjust them after moving from an upright, to a more aero position while riding. So I thought I’d give the Mantis a try—and so far, I’ve not been disappointed. The mirrors are really clear, there’s negligible vibration, and most importantly, once it’s set up, there’s little need to adjust anything when moving from upright to a lower position on the bars.

A couple of added bonuses: once the base mount is attached to the helmet, the removable Mantis unit attaches quickly, and there’s a simple marking system that helps you find your preferred setup for future rides.

All in all, I’m quite happy with this helmet mirror and looking forward to many long rides with it.

— Dom

Brock

I received mine late Wednesday — shipping was pretty quick (it came from Ottawa). The training videos were well done overall. Because they emphasize how important the helmet mount position is (think GoPro-style mount), the full setup took me closer to 40 minutes even though the videos total around 20. I did it solo, and I can see how their two-person recommended install would make positioning easier.

After the minimum 24 hours setting time, I was keen to try it, so I took it for a ride around the block. First impressions are surprisingly positive. The mirrors are stable, very clear (I forgot to peel the protective film at first…), and the whole thing is really light — lighter than my longtime single-mirror setup.

The big claim — the two-position setup working for both upright and more aero riding — actually holds up. I did see a bit of my shoulder in the upper mirror when down low, but it was maybe 10% of the view and didn’t affect visibility. They mention this might happen, so it wasn’t unexpected. I can see how this will save me some energy on longer rides, not having to pop up as often to check behind me.

Looking forward to giving it a proper test ride once the weather cooperates. As a small bonus, it’s nice knowing this is a Canadian innovation, made in Canada. Well done, Mantis Mirror.

— Brock
