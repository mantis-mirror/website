The Challenge

Why riders still glance over their shoulder

The roads are busier than ever, distractions are everywhere, and traditional mirrors haven’t kept up with the pace of modern cycling.

Escalating distractions

Biking has become less safe because of increased road traffic and distracted driving. When you can’t see what’s behind you, every ride can feel like an accident waiting to happen.

Staggering statistics

Nearly 400,000 preventable bicycle accidents occur every year, with many caused by vehicles approaching from the rear. Bike riding fatalities continue to rise, with almost 1,300 deaths in the U.S. alone during 2020.

Traditional mirrors fall short

Older single-mirror solutions limit visibility. They don’t adapt as you shift your position, forcing riders to readjust mid-ride — a risky move when focus should stay on the road ahead.

The human cost

Shoulder checks, twisting, and turning disrupt cadence, waste energy, and impact balance. Riders deserve a smarter way to stay informed about what’s coming up behind them.
