Safety in focus

The Problem

Increasing traffic and distracted driving leave riders exposed to the unexpected.

Biking should feel freeing, yet modern roads tell a different story. More vehicles and more distractions mean riders are often unaware of what is approaching from behind. Knowing what is around you is essential for confident riding.

Nearly 400,000 preventable bicycle accidents occur every year, many caused by rear-approaching vehicles. Bike riding fatalities have continued to rise, with nearly 1,300 deaths in 2020 in the U.S. alone. Bicycle deaths accounted for 2% of all motor-vehicle traffic fatalities (National Safety Council 2022).

Traditional bicycle mirrors try to help, but their single mirror design limits visibility. Riders have to reposition the mirror—or themselves—just to get a usable view. Doing that in traffic is distracting and dangerous.

The science and stats are clear: there must be a better way to protect riders and preserve their flow. That solution is the Mantis™ Mirror System.

Why traditional mirrors fall short

  • Single-mirror designs leave massive blind spots and require constant readjustment mid-ride.
  • Forcing riders to shoulder-check disrupts cadence, drains energy, and compromises balance.
  • Poor mounting solutions vibrate loose, discouraging riders from relying on mirrors at all.
